AI and Academic Integrity
Syracuse University's guidelines regarding the use of AI has proven to be vague.

By Jaden Wilson, Jenn Wybieracki, Jes√∫s Feliciano-Batista

Published : 27th July 2024

SU Campus

The use of artificial intelligence has become a controversial topic in classrooms everywhere. Taking this into consideration, Syracuse University established in their Code of Academic Integrity that each professor has the right to decide how to tackle the subject.

This decision has led to confusion for graduate students, as they look for more concrete direction from the school. For starters, graduate students want more liberty from the university to explore AI in their classes.

As the previous chart shows, seven out of the 18 polled graduate students are open to using AI, while another four strongly agree with the use of AI. However, this has not stoped the same students from believing that using AI tools such as ChatGPT should be considered cheating.

Out of the 18 graduate students who selected they would support AI in the classroom, more than half pointed ChatGPT out as an exception. Despite ChatGPT being the most popular AI tool students can use for their classes, students do not seem to support it.

ChatGPT is divisive for grad students

The idea of AI in SU is very divisive for graduate students. As a result, there is a clear space for Syracuse leadership to establish guidelines.

Even with the space for guidelines, students are not completely sold on what role SU should play in placing limits on AI. The previous chart demonstrates that more than half of students are on the fence regarding the subject.

The conversation regarding AI has come a long way in the past years, but it has a long way to go. SU needs to determine how to move forward with this topics, and their students are letting them know.
